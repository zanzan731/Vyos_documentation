# v5.15 Firewall Rules

This file summarizes the effective rules in `v5.15.conf` with the numbering convention:

- `1`: state/return traffic
- `50` series: segment -> internet egress (eth0)
- `100` series: WAN/public -> DMZ public services
- `200` series: INTERNAL clients (`eth2`) -> allowed services only
- `300` series: DMZ east-west/admin/monitoring
- `400` series: IPV6ONLY clients (`eth3`) -> allowed services only

## Simple Summary

In plain terms, this firewall follows a default-deny model: if a connection is not listed below, it is blocked.

What is allowed:

- Internal hosts can reach the Internet, DMZ DNS, the DMZ web services they need, monitoring, and AD services.
- DMZ hosts can reach the Internet and can use the services hosted inside the DMZ, including scriptum, DNS, wg-portal, monitoring, and AD.
- IPv6-only hosts can reach the Internet and the same allowed DMZ services as internal hosts, using IPv6.
- DHCPv6 renewals from DMZ and IPv6-only hosts are allowed to the router.
- WireGuard hosts can SSH or RDP to DMZ, internal, and IPv6-only machines.
- Pings are allowed across routed segments for IPv4 and IPv6, including ping to VyOS from internal and DMZ.
- VyOS IPv6 output explicitly allows ICMPv6 so neighbor discovery and routed IPv6 reachability remain reliable.
- Internal and DMZ users can reach the public IP of allowed services through hairpin NAT.

What is blocked:

- Random inbound traffic from the Internet is blocked unless there is a specific WAN rule for it.
- SSH and RDP between machines in the same segment are blocked, except when the traffic comes from WireGuard.
- Internal hosts cannot access services that are not explicitly listed in the rules.
- IPv6-only hosts cannot access anything outside the allowed IPv6 rules.
